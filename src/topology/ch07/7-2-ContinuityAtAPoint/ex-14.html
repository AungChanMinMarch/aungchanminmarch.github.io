--- title: "Ex 14" ---
Consider the following topology defined on $X = \{a,b,c,d\}$: $$\Tau = \{ X, \emptyset, \{a\}, \{b\}, \{a,b\}, \{b,c,d\} \}$$ Let the function $f:X \to X$ be defined by the adjoining diagram.
  1. Show that $f$ is not continuous at $c$.
  2. Show that $f$ is continuous at $d$.

  1. $f(c) = b$. The open sets containing $f(c) = b$ are $X$, $\{b\}$, $\{a, b\}$ and $\{b,c,d \}$.

    $\{b \}$ is an open set containing $f(c)$ but $\inverse{f}{\{ b\}} = \{a, c\}$

    The open sets containing $c$ are $X$ and $\{b,c,d\}$.

    Thus there is no open sets containing $c$ which is contained in $\{a,c\}$.

    Thus $f$ is not continuous at $c$.

  2. $f(d) = c$. The open sets containing $f(d) = c$ are $X$ and $\{b,c,d\}$.

    \begin{align} \inverse{f}{X} &= \{a,c,d,b\} = X \\ \inverse{f}{\{b,c,d\}} &= \{a,c,d,b\} = X \\ \end{align}

    $\{b,c,d\}$ is an open set containing $d$ and $\{b,c,d\}$ is a superset of $X$.

    Thus $f$ is continuous at $d$.
