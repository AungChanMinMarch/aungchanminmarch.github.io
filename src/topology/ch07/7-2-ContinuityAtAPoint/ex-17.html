--- title: "Ex 17 = Th 7.5" ---
Prove Theorem 7.5: Let $X$ and $Y$ be topological spaces. Then a function $f: X \to Y$ is continuous if and only if it is continuous at every point $p \in X$.

Suppose $f: X \to Y$ is continuous.

Let $p$ be any point of $X$.

Let $H$ be an open subset of $Y$ containing $f(p)$.

Since $f$ is continuous, $\inverse{f}{H}$ is an open subset of $X$.

Since $f(p) \in H$, $p \in \inverse{f}{H}$.

Hence $\inverse{f}{H}$ is an open set containing $p$. Thus $f$ is continuous at $p$

Now suppose $f$ is continuous at every point $p \in X$.

Let $G$ be an open subset of $Y$. We need to prove $\inverse{f}{G}$ is open.

Since $f$ is continuous at every point $p \in X$, $f$ is continuous at every point $p \in \inverse{f}{H}$.

Thus for every $p \in \inverse{f}{G}$, there exists an open set $G_p \subset X$ such that $p \in G_p \subset \inverse{f}{G}$.

Since $\inverse{f}{G} = \cup \{G_p : p \in \inverse{f}{G} \}$, $\inverse{f}{G}$ is union of open subsets of $X$.

Thus $\inverse{f}{G}$ is open. $f$ is continuous.
