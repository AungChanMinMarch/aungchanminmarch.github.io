--- title: "Ex 18 = proposition 7.6" ---
Prove Proposition 7.6: If a function $f:X \to Y$ is continuous at $p \in X$, then it is sequentially continuous at $p$.

Let $N$ be a neighborhoood of $f(p)$.

Since $f$ is continuous at $p$, $\inverse{f}{N}$ is a neighborhood of $p$.

Let $M = \inverse{f}{N}$

Then $M$ is a neighborhood of $p$>

Hence, if a sequence $\seq{a_n} \to p$, $M$ contains almost all the terms of the sequence $\seq{a_1, a_2, \cdots }$

\begin{align} a_n &\in M \text{ for almost all } n \\ f(a_n) &\in f[M] = f[\inverse{f}{N}] = N \text{ for almost all } n \\ \therefore f(a_n) &\in N \text{ for almost all } n \end{align}

Since $N$ is neighborhoood of $f(p)$ and $f(a_n) \in N$ for almost all $n$, $\seq{f(a_n)}$ converges to $f(p)$.

Thus $f$ is sequentially continuous at $p$.
