Consider the following topologies on $X = \{a,b,c,d\}$ and $Y = \{x,y,z, w\}$ respectively:
$$\topologyTau = \{X, \emptyset, \{a\}, \{a,b\}, \{a,b,c\} \}$$ $$\topologyTau^* = \{ Y, \emptyset, \{x\}, \{y\}, \{x,y\}, \{y,z,w\} \}$$ Also consider the functions f:X->Y and g:Xâ€”>Y defined by the diagrams below:

Are $f$ and $g$ continuous?

Since $\topologyTau^* = \{ Y, \emptyset, \{x\}, \{y\}, \{x,y\}, \{y,z,w\} \}$,

\begin{align*} Y &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[Y] = \{a,b,c,d\} = X &\in \topologyTau \\ \emptyset &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\emptyset] = \emptyset &\in \topologyTau \\ \{x\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{x\}] = \emptyset &\in \topologyTau \\ \{y\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{y\}] = \{a\} &\in \topologyTau \\ \{x,y\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{x,y\}] = \{a\} &\in \topologyTau \\ \{y,z,w\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{y,z,w\}] = \{a,b,c,d\} = X &\in \topologyTau \\ \end{align*} $$ \text{Hence } H \in \topologyTau^* \text{ implies } f^{-1}[H] \in \topologyTau$$ \begin{align*} Y &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & g^{-1}[Y] = \{a,b,c,d\} = X &\in \topologyTau \\ \emptyset &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\emptyset] = \emptyset &\in \topologyTau \\ \{x\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{x\}] = \{a,b\} &\in \topologyTau \\ \{y\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{y\}] = \emptyset &\in \topologyTau \\ \{x,y\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{x,y\}] = \{a, y\} &\in \topologyTau \\ \{y,z,w\} &\in \topologyTau^* \implies & f^{-1}[\{y,z,w\}] = \{c,d\} &\notin \topologyTau \\ \end{align*}

Hence the function $g$ is not continuous since $\{y, z, w \} \in \topologyTau^*$ but $g^{-1}[\{y,z,w\}] = \{c,d\} \notin \topologyTau$.
