--- title: "Ex 12" ---
Prove: Let $f:(X, \Tau) \to (Y,\Tau^*)$ be continuous. Then $f_A:(A, \Tau_A) \to (Y, \Tau^*)$ is continuous, where $A \subset X$ and $f_A$ is the restriction of $f$ to $A$.

Let $H$ be $\Tau^*$-open set of $Y$.

Since $f$ is $\Tau$-$\Tau^*$ continuous, $\inverse{f}{H} \in \Tau$

By the definition of relative topology, we get $\inverse{f}{H} \cap A \in \Tau_A$

Since $\inverse{f_A}{H} = \inverse{f}{H} \cap A$, we have $\inverse{f_A}{H} \in \Tau_A$.

Thus $f_A$ is $\Tau_A$-$\Tau^*$ continuous.
