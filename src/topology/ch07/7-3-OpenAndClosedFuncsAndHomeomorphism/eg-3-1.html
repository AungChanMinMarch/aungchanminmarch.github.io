--- title: "Eg 3.1" ---
Let $X = (—1,1)$. Show that the function $f:X \to \R$ defined by $f(x) = \tan \frac{1}{2} \pi x$ is homeomorphic.

Let $X = (-1, 1)$.

Let $f:X \to \R$ be defined by $f(x) = \tan \frac{1}{2} \pi x$

Suppose $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$, where $x_1, x_2 \in X$.

\begin{align} f(x_1) &= f(x_2)\\ \tan \frac{1}{2} \pi x_1 &= \tan \frac{1}{2} \pi x_2\\ x_1 &= x_2\\ \end{align} Thus $f$ is one-one.

For any $y \in \R$, there exists $x = \frac{2}{\pi} \tan^{-1} y \in X$ such that $f(x) = \tan (\frac{1}{2} \pi x) = \tan(\frac{1}{2} \pi \frac{2}{\pi} \tan^{-1} y) = \tan(\tan^{-1} y) = y$.

Thus $f$ is onto.

Since $f(x) = \tan \frac{1}{2} \pi x$, it is obvious that $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are continuous.

Hence the real line $\R$ and the open interval $(—1,1)$ are homeomorphic and $f$ is homeomorphism.
