--- title: "Ex 25" ---
Let $f:(X,\Tau) \to (Y,\Tau^*)$ be a homeomorphism and let $(A, \Tau_A)$ be any subspace of $(X, \Tau)$. Show that $f_A:(A,T_A) \to (B, \Tau_B^*)$ is also a homeomorphism where $f_A$ is the restriction of $f$ to $A$, $f[A] = B$, and $\Tau_B^*$ is the relative topology on B.

Let $f:(X,\Tau) \to (Y,\Tau^*)$ be a homeomorphism.

Let $(A, \Tau_A)$ be any subspace of $(X, \Tau)$.

Let $f[A] = B$.

Since $f$ is one-one and onto, $f_A:A \to B$ is clearly one-one and onto.

We need to prove $f_A$ is bicontinuous, i.e. $f_A$ is open and continuous.

Let $H \in \Tau_B^*$.

Then there exist $G \in \Tau^*$ such that $H=G \cap B$

\begin{align} \inverse{f_A}{H} &= \inverse{f}{H} \\ &= \inverse{f}{G \cap B} \\ &= \inverse{f}{G} \cap \inverse{f}{B} \\ &= \inverse{f}{G} \cap A \end{align}

Since $f:(X, \Tau) \to (T, \Tau^*)$ is continuous and $G \in \Tau^*$, $\inverse{f}{G} \in \Tau$.

Thus $\inverse{f_A}{H} = \inverse{f}{G} \cap A \in \Tau_A$.

Since $H \in \Tau_B^*$ implies $\inverse{f_A}{H} \in \Tau_A$, $f_A$ $\Tau_A$-$\Tau_B^*$ continuous.

Now we need to prove $f_A:(A,T_A) \to (B, \Tau_B^*)$ is open.

Let $G \in \Tau_A$.

Then there exist $D \in \Tau$ such that $D \cap A = G$

\begin{align} f_A[G] &= f[G] \\ &= f[D \cap A] \\ &= f[D] \cap f[A] \\ &= f[D] \cap B \end{align}

Since $f:(X, \Tau) \to (T, \Tau^*)$ is open and $D \in \Tau$, $f[D] \in \Tau^*$.

Therefore $f_A[G] = f[D] \cap B \in \Tau_B^*$.

Since $G \in \Tau_A$ implies $f_A[G] \in \Tau_B^*$, $f:(A, \Tau_A) \to (B, \Tau_B^*)$ is open. Thus $f_A$ is bicontinuous and hence a homeomorphism
