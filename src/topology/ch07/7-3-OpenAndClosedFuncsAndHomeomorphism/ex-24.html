--- title: "Ex 24" ---
Let $f:(X, \Tau) \to (Y, \Tau^*)$ be one-one and open, let $A \subset X$, and let $f[A] = B$. Show that the function $f_A:(A, \Tau_A) \to (B, \Tau_B^*)$ is also one-one and open, where $f_A$ denotes the restriction of $f$ to $A$, and $\Tau_A$ and $\Tau_B^*$ are the relative topologies.

We want to prove $f_A:A \to B$ is one-one.

Suppose $f_A(x_1) = f_A(x_2)$, where $x_1, x_2 \in A$.

Since $x_1, x_2 \in A$, by the definition of restriction fuction, $f(x_1) = f_A(x_1)$ and $f(x_2) = f_A(x_2)$.

Therefore $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$.

Since $f:X \to Y$ is continuous, $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$ implies $x_1 = x_2$

Thus $f_A$ is one-one.

Now we shall prove $f_A:(A, \Tau_A) \to (B, \Tau_B^*)$ is open.

Let $G$ be a $\Tau_A$-open set.

Then by the definition of relative topology, there exist $H \in \Tau$ such $G = H \cap A$.

\begin{align} f_A[G] &= f[G] \\ &= f[H \cap A] \\ &= f[H] \cap f[A] \\ &= f[H] \cap B \end{align}

Since $f:(X, \Tau) \to (Y, \Tau^*)$ is open and $H \in \Tau$,$f[H] \in \Tau^*$.

Since $f_A[G] = f[H] \cap B$ and $f[H] \in \TauStar$, we have $f_A[G] \in \Tau_B^*$.

Since $G \in \Tau_A$ implies $f_A[G] \in \Tau_B^*$, $f_A:(A, \Tau_A) \to (B, \Tau_B^*)$ is open.
