--- title: "Ex 22" ---
Show that the closed interval $A = [a,b]$ is homeomorphic to the closed unit interval $I=[0,1]$.

Let $A=[a,b]$ and $I=[0,1]$.

Let $f:I \to A$ be defined by $f(x)= a + (b-a)x$

Suppose $f(x_1) = f(x_2)$, where $x_1, x_2 \in A$.

\begin{align} f(x_1) &= f(x_2) \\ a + (b-a)x_1 &= a + (b-a)x_2 \\ (b-a)x_1 &= (b-a)x_2 \\ x_1 &= x_2 \end{align}

Thus $f$ is one-one.

Let $y \in A$

\begin{array}{rcl} a \leq & y &\leq b \\ 0 \leq & y - a &\leq b -a \\ 0 \leq & \frac{y - a}{b-a} &\leq 1 (\because b -a > 0) \\ \end{array}

Thus for any $y \in A=[a,b]$, there exists $x = \frac{y - a}{b-a} \in I=[0,1]$ such that $f(x)=f(\frac{y - a}{b-a}) = a + (b-a) \frac{y - a}{b-a} = a + y -a = y$.

Thus $f$ is onto and hence $f$ is bijective.

Clearly, $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are continuous and hence $f$ is bicontinuous.

Thus $f$ is homeomorphism and $A$ is homeomorphic to $I$.
