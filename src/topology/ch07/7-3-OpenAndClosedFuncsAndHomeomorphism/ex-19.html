--- title: "Ex 19" ---
Give an example of a real function such that $f$ is continuous and closed, but not open.

Let $H$ be an open subset of $\R$.

Since $f(x) = 1$ for all $x \in \R$, $$ f^{-1}[H] = \begin{cases} \R &\mbox{ if } 1 \in H \\ \emptyset &\mbox{ if } 1 \notin H \end{cases}$$

since $\R$ and $\emptyset$ are open, $f^{-1}[H]$ is open.

Thus $f$ is continuous.

Let $f:\R \to \R$ be defined by $f(x) = 1$.

Since $f[A] = \{1\}$ for any $A \subset \R$, $A \ne \emptyset$.

Hence $f[A]$ is closed for any closed set $A$. Thus $f$ is closed.

Suppose $A$ be a nonempty open set. Then $f[A]=\{1\}$ is not open. Hence $f$ is not open.
