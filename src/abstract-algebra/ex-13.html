If $G$ is a group and $N \triangleleft G$ is such that $a b a^{-1} b^{-1} \in N$ for all $a,b \in G$, prove that $G / N$ is abelian.
$$G / N = \{ Na \mid a \in G \}$$

Take any $X,Y \in G/N$.

Let $X = Na, Y= Nb$, where $a,b \in G$.

$$\begin{align*} \mbox{Since } a b a^{-1} b^{-1} &\in N \\ a b a^{-1} b^{-1} &= n_1 \mbox{ for some } n_1 \in N \\ (a b a^{-1} b^{-1})b &= n_1 b\\ a b a^{-1} &= n_1 b\\ (a b a^{-1})a &= n_1 b a\\ a b &= n_1 b a\\ n_2 (a b) &= (n_2 n_1) b a \mbox{ where } n_2 \in N \\ n_2 (a b) &= n_3 b a \mbox{ where } n_2 n_1 = n_3 \in N \\ N(ab) &= N(ba)\\ NaNb &= Nb Na\\ XY &= YX \end{align*}$$

Hence $G / N$ is abelian.
