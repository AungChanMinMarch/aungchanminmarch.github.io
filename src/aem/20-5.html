Fitting A Straight Line

The straight line $y=a+bx$ should be fitted through the given points $(x_1, y_1), \dots, (x_n, y_n)$ so that the sum of the squares of the distances of those points from the straight line is minimum, where the distance is measured in the vertical direction (the y-direction).

The point on the line with abscissa $x_j$ has the ordinate $a+bx_j$. Hence its vertical distance from $(x_j, y_j)$ is $\abs{y_j - a - b x_j}$ and therefore the sum of the squares is $q= \Sum_{j=1}^{n} (y_j - a - b x_j)^2$

$q$ depends on $a$ and $b$. A necessary condition for $q$ to be minimum is $\partialwrt[q]{a} = 0 $ and $\partialwrt[q]{b} = 0 $

$$\begin{align} \partialwrt[q]{a} &= 0 \\ \partialwrt{a} \bracket{\Sum_{j=1}^{n} (y_j - a - b x_j)^2} &= 0 \\ \Sum_{j=1}^{n} \partialwrt{a} \bracket{(y_j - a - b x_j)^2} &= 0 \\ \Sum_{j=1}^{n} -2(y_j - a - b x_j) &= 0 \\ -2\Sum_{j=1}^{n} (y_j - a - b x_j) &= 0 \\ -\Sum_{j=1}^{n} (y_j - a - b x_j) &= 0 \\ -\Sum_{j=1}^{n} y_j + \Sum_{j=1}^{n} a + \Sum_{j=1}^{n} b x_j &= 0 \\ -\Sum_{j=1}^{n} y_j + an + b\Sum_{j=1}^{n} x_j &= 0 \\ an + b\Sum_{j=1}^{n} x_j &= \Sum_{j=1}^{n} y_j \tag{1}\\ \end{align}$$ Similarly, we get $$a \sum x_j + b \sum {x_j}^2 = \sum x_j y_j \tag{2}$$ Equation (1) and (2) are called the normal equations .
