--- title: "Theorem 7.13" order: 13 parent: uniformConvergenceAndContinuity ---

Suppose $K$ is compact, and

  1. $\{f_n\}$ is a sequence of continuous functions on $K$,
  2. $\{f_n\}$ converges pointwise to a continuous function $f$ on $K$.
  3. $f_n(x) \geq f_{n+1}(x)$ for all $x \in K, n=1,2,3, \dots$
Then $f_n \to f$ uniformly on $K$.

Put $g_n = f_n -f$ and $\ep >0$.

Let $K_n = \{x \in K \mid g_n(x) \geq \ep \}$

Since $f_n$ and $f$ are continuous on $K$, $g_n$ is continuous on $K$.

Hence by 4.8, $K_n$ is closed. Hence by 2.35, $K_n$ is compact.

$$\begin{align*} \mbox{For all } x \in K, & n=1,2,3, \dots, \\ \mbox{Since } f_n(x) &\geq f_{n+1}(x) \\ f_n(x) -f(x) &\geq f_{n+1}(x) -f(x)\\ (f_n-f)(x) &\geq (f_{n+1}-f)(x) \\ g_n(x) &\geq g_{n+1}(x)\\ \mbox{If } x &\in K_{n+1},\\ g_{n+1}(x) &\geq \ep \\ g_n(x) \geq g_{n+1}(x) &\geq \ep \\ g_n(x) &\geq \ep\\ x &\in K_n \\ K_{n+1} &\subset K_n \end{align*}$$ $$\begin{align*} \limit g_n(x) &= \limit (f_n-f)(x) \\ &= \limit (f_n(x) - f(x))\\ &= \limit f_n(x)- \limit f(x) \\ &= f(x) - f(x) \\ &= 0 \\ \therefore g_n &\to 0 \mbox{ pointwise. } \end{align*}$$

We have to prove that $g_n \to 0$ uniformly. Fix $x \in K$. Since $g_n(x) \to 0$, $x \notin K_n$ if $n$ is sufficiently large. Thus $x \notin \cap K_n$ and $\cap K_n$ is empty.

Hence 2.36, there exists integer $N$ such that $K_N$ is empty.

$$\begin{align*} \mbox{Since $K_{n+1}\subset K_n$ and $K_N$ is empty, } n \geq N &\mbox{ implies }& K_n &= \phi \\ x \in K, n \geq N &\mbox{ implies }& g_n(x) &< \ep \\ \end{align*}$$

Therefore there exists integer $N$ such that $x \in K$ and $n \geq N$ implies that $\abs{f_n(x) - f(x)} < \ep$

$f_n \to f$ uniformly.
