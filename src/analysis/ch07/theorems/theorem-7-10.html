--- title: Theorem 7.10 parent: uniformConvergence order: 10 ---

Suppose $\{f_n\}$ is a sequence of functions defined on $E$, and suppose $\abs{f_n(x)} \leq M_n \ (x\in E, n=1,2,3, \dots)$

Then $\sum f_n$ converges uniformly on $E$ if $\sum M_n$ converges.

Let $\{s_n\}$ be the sequence of the partial sums defined by $\Sum_{i=1}^{n} f_i(x) = s_n(x)$.

$$\begin{align*} n > m &\mbox{ implies } & \abs{s_n(x) - s_m(x)} &= \abs{\Sum_{i=1}^{n} f_i(x) - \Sum_{i=1}^{m} f_i(x)} \\ &&&= \abs{\Sum_{i=m+1}^{n} f_i(x)} \\ n > m &\mbox{ implies }& \abs{s_n(x) - s_m(x)} &\leq \Sum_{i=m+1}^n \abs{f_i(x)}\\ n > m, x \in E &\mbox{ implies }& \abs{s_n(x) - s_m(x)} &\leq \Sum_{i=m+1}^{n} M_i \end{align*}$$

Since $\sum M_n$ converges, by {% a 3.22 %}, for any $\ep > 0 $, there exists integer $N$ such that

$$\begin{align*} n \geq m \geq N \mbox{ implies that } \abs{\sum_{i=m}^n M_i} &\leq \ep\\ \abs{s_n(x) - s_m(x)} \leq \Sum_{i=m+1}^{n} M_i \leq \sum_{i=m}^n M_i &\leq \ep \end{align*}$$ $$n \geq m \geq N, x \in E \mbox{ implies } \abs{s_n(x) - s_m(x)} \leq \ep $$

Hence by {% a '7.8' %} the sequence $\{s_n\}$ converges uniformly. Since sequence of partial sums converge uniformly, $\sum f_n$ converges uniformly.
