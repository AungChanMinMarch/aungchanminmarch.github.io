--- title: "Theorem 7.16" parent: uniformConvergenceAndIntegration order: 16 ---

Let $\alpha$ be monotonically increasing on $[a,b]$. Suppose $f_n \in \mathscr{R}(\alpha)$ on $[a,b]$, for $n=1,2,3,\dots$ and suppose $f_n \to f$ uniformly on $[a,b]$. Then $f \in \riemannR(\alpha)$ on $[a,b]$, and $$\int_a^b f d\alpha = \limit \int_a^b f_n d\alpha.$$

It suffices to prove this for real $f_n$.

Let $\ep_n = \sup\limits_{a \leq x \leq b} \abs{f_n(x) - f(x)}$.

\mbox{Then } && \abs{f_n(x) - f(x)} &\leq& \ep_n \\ -\ep_n &\leq& f_n(x) - f(x) &\leq& \ep_n \\ -f_n(x)-\ep_n &\leq& - f(x) &\leq& -f_n(x) + \ep_n \\ f_n(x)+\ep_n &\geq& f(x) &\geq& f_n(x) - \ep_n \\ f_n(x)-\ep_n &\leq& f(x) &\leq& f_n(x) + \ep_n
$$ \mbox{Hence } \int_a^b (f_n(x)-\ep_n) d\alpha \leq \lowint_a^b f(x) d\alpha \leq \upint_a^b f(x) d\alpha \leq \int_a^b(f_n(x) + \ep_n) d\alpha \tag{1}$$ $$\begin{align*} 0 \leq \upint_a^b f(x) d\alpha - \lowint_a^b f(x) d\alpha &\leq \int_a^b (f_n(x)+\ep_n) d\alpha - \int_a^b (f_n(x)-\ep_n) d\alpha \\ &= \int_a^b f_n(x) d\alpha + \int_a^b \ep_n d\alpha - \int_a^b f_n(x) d\alpha + \int_a^b \ep_n d\alpha \\ &= 2\ep_n (\alpha(b) -\alpha(b)\\ \therefore0 \leq \upint_a^b f(x) d\alpha - \lowint_a^b f(x) d\alpha &\leq 2\ep_n (\alpha(b) -\alpha(b)\\ \end{align*}$$

Since $f_n \to f$ uniformly, by 7.9, $\ep_n \to 0$ as $n\to \infty$.

$$\mbox{Hence } \upint_a^b f(x) d\alpha - \lowint_a^b f(x) d\alpha = 0$$

Therefore $f \in \riemannR(\alpha)$.

$$ \mbox{Now (1) gives } \int_a^b (f_n(x)-\ep_n) d\alpha \leq \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha \leq \int_a^b(f_n(x) + \ep_n) d\alpha$$ $$\begin{align*} \mbox{Hence }\int_a^b (f_n(x)-\ep_n) d\alpha &\leq \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha \\ \int_a^b f_n(x) d \alpha - \ep_n (\alpha(b) - \alpha(a)) &\leq \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha \\ \int_a^b f_n(x) d \alpha - \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha &\leq \ep_n (\alpha(b) - \alpha(a)) \tag{2} \end{align*}$$ $$\begin{align*} \mbox{Also } \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha &\leq \int_a^b(f_n(x) + \ep_n) d\alpha \\ \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha &\leq \int_a^b f_n(x) d\alpha + \ep_n(\alpha(b) -\alpha(a)) \\ -\ep_n(\alpha(b) - \alpha(a)) &\leq \int_a^b f_n(x) d\alpha - \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha \tag{3} \end{align*}$$ $$\mbox{(2) and (3) gives } \abs{\int_a^b f_n(x) d\alpha - \int_a^b f(x) d\alpha} \leq \ep_n(\alpha(b) - \alpha(a))$$

Since $f_n \to f$ uniformly, by 7.9, $\ep_n \to 0$ as $n\to \infty$.

$$\mbox{Therefore} \int_a^b f d\alpha = \limit \int_a^b f_n d\alpha$$

Corollary

If $f_n \in \riemannR(\alpha)$ on $[a,b]$ and if $f(x)=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} f_n(x)$ ($a\leq x \leq b$), the series converging uniformly on $[a,b]$, then $$\int_a^b f d\alpha = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \int_a^b f_n d\alpha$$

Since $f(x)=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{\infty} f_i(x)$ converges uniformly on $[a,b]$, the sequence of partial sums $\{s_n\}$ defined by $s_n(x) = \sum\limits_{i=1}^n f_i(x)$ converges uniformly to $f$ on $[a,b]$.

$$\begin{align*} \int_a^b f d\alpha &= \limit \int_a^b s_n d\alpha \\ &= \limit \int_a^b \sum\limits_{i=1}^n f_i d\alpha \\ &= \limit \sum\limits_{i=1}^n \int_a^b f_i d\alpha \ (\because f_n \in \riemannR(\alpha)) \\ &= \sum\limits_{i=1}^{\infty} \int_a^b f_i d\alpha \\ \end{align*}$$
