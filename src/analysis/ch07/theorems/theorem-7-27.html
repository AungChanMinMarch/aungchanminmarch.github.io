--- title: "Corollary 7.27: THE STONE-WEIERSTRASS THEOREM" order: 27 parent: theStoneWeierstrassTheorem ---
For every interval $[-a,a]$ there is a sequence of real polynomials $P_n$ such that $P_n(0)=0$ such that $$\limit P_n(x) =\mod{x}$$ uniformly on $[-a,a]$.

By theorem 7.26, since $\mod{x}$ is continuous on $[-a,a]$, there exists a sequence of polynomial $P^{*}_n$ such that $\limit P^{*}_n(x) = \mod{x}$ uniformly on $[a,b]$.

$P^{*}_n(x) \to 0$ as $n\to \infty$.

Let $P_n(x) = P^{*}_n(x) - P^{*}_n(0)$.

Then $P_n(0) = P^{*}_n(0) - P^{*}_n(0) = 0$.

$$\begin{align*} \limit P_n(x) &= \limit (P^{*}_n(x) - P^{*}_n(0)) \\ &= \limit P^{*}_n(x) - \limit P^{*}_n(0)\\ &= \mod{x} - 0 \\ &= \mod{x} \end{align*}$$

Since $\limit P^{*}_n(x) = \mod{x}$ uniformly on $[a,b]$, $\limit P_n(x) = \mod{x}$ uniformly on $[a,b]$.
