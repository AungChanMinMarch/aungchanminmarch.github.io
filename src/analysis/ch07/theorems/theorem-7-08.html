--- title: Theorem 7.8 parent: uniformConvergence order: 8 ---

The sequence of functions $\{f_n\}$, defined on $E$, converges uniformly on $E$ if and only if for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists an integer $N$ such that $m \geq N, n\geq N, x \in E$ implies $\mod{f_n(x)-f_m(x)} \leq \varepsilon$

Suppose $\{f_n\}$ converges uniformly on $E$.

Let $\ep > 0$ and $f$ be the limit function.

Then there exists an integer $N$ such that $n\geq N, x \in E$ implies $\abs{f_n(x)-f(x)} \leq \frac{\varepsilon}{2}$
$$\begin{align} \abs{f_n(x)-f_m(x)} &= \abs{f_n(x)-f(x)+f(x)-f_m(x)}\\ &\leq \abs{f_n(x)-f(x)} + \abs{f_m(x)-f(x)} \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} n\geq N, x \in E \ \text{ implies } \abs{f_n(x)-f(x)} &\leq \frac{\ep}{2}\\ m\geq N, x \in E \ \text{ implies } \abs{f_m(x)-f(x)} &\leq \frac{\ep}{2}\\ \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} \mbox{Hence } n\geq N, m &\geq N, x \in E \ \text{ implies } \\ \abs{f_n(x)-f_m(x)} &\leq \abs{f_n(x)-f(x)} + \abs{f_m(x)-f(x)}\\ &\leq \frac{\varepsilon}{2} + \frac{\varepsilon}{2} = \varepsilon \end{align}$$

Therefore there exists an integer $N$ such that $n\geq N, m \geq N, x \in E$ implies $\abs{f_n(x)-f_m(x)} \leq \varepsilon$

Now suppose that for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists an integer $N$ such that $$m \geq N, n\geq N, x \in E \mbox{ implies } \mod{f_n(x)-f_m(x)} \leq \varepsilon \tag{1}$$

Then, by {% a 3.11 %}, for every $x$, the sequence $\{f_n(x)\}$ converges to the limit, say $f(x)$. Thus $f_n \to f$ pointwise. We need to prove $f_n \to f$ uniformly.

Let $\varepsilon > 0$ be given. Choose an integer $N$ such that (1) holds.

As $m \to \infty$, $f_m(x) \to f(x)$

Hence fixing $n$ and letting $m \to \infty$ in (1) gives $n\geq N, x \in E$ implies $\mod{f_n(x)-f(x)} \leq \varepsilon$

Therefore $\{f_n\}$ converges uniformly on $E$.

The proof is completed.
