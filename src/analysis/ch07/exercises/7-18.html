Let $\{f_n\}$ be a uniformly bounded sequence of functions which are Riemann-integrable on $[a,b]$, and put $$F_n(x) = \int_a^x f_n(t) dt (a \leq x \leq b)$$ Prove that there exists a subsequence $\{F_{n_k}\}$ which converges uniformly on $[a,b]$.

Since $\{f_n\}$ is a uniformly bounded sequence of functions, there is a number $M$ such that $\abs{f_n(x)} < M$ for all $n$ and $x\in [a,b]$. $$\begin{align*} \abs{F_n(x)} &= \abs{\int_a^x f_n(t) dt}\\ &\leq \int_a^x \abs{f_n(t)} dt \\ &< \int_a^x M dt \\ &= M (x-a) \\ &\leq M(b-a)\\ \therefore \abs{F_n(x)} &< M(b-a) \mbox{ for all $n$ and $x$} \end{align*}$$ Hence $\{F_n\}$ is uniformly bounded.

Given $\ep > 0$, let $\delta = \dfrac{\ep}{M}$.
Then $x,y \in [a,b], x < y$ and $\abs{y-x} < \delta$ implies that $$\begin{align*} \abs{F_n(y) - F_n(x)} &= \abs{\int_a^y f_n(t) dt - \int_a^x f_n(t) dt}\\ &= \abs{\int_a^x f_n(t) dt + \int_x^y f_n(t) dt - \int_a^x f_n(t) dt}\\ &= \abs{\int_x^y f_n(t) dt}\\ &\leq \int_x^y \abs{f_n(t)} dt\\ &< \int_x^y M dt\\ &= M(y-x) \\ &< M\delta \\ &= \ep \\ \therefore \abs{F_n(y) - F_n(x)} < \ep \end{align*}$$ Hence $\{F_n\}$ is equicontinuous.

Hence by 7.25, there exists a subsequence $\{F_{n_k}\}$ which converges uniformly on $[a,b]$.
