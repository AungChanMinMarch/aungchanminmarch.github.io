--- title: Theorem 3.51 order: 51 ---
If the series $\sum a_n, \sum b_n, \sum c_n$ converge to $A$, $B$, $C$, and $c_n = a_0 b_n + ... + a_n b_0$, then $C = AB$.
A detailed and valid proof can be found as an aapplication of theorem 8.2(Check here {% a 8.2 %}) $$\begin{align} AB &= (\sum a_n) (\sum b_n) \\ &= (a_0 + a_1 + a_2 + \dots ) (b_0 + b_1 + b_2 + \dots) \\ &= a_0 b_0 + a_0 b_1 +a_1 b_0 + a_0 b_2 + a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_0 + \dots \\ &= c_0 + c_1 + c_2 + \dots \\ &= C \end{align}$$
