The Trigonometric Functoins

Let us define $$C(x) = \frac{1}{2} [E(ix)+ E(-ix)]$$ $$S(x) = \frac{1}{2i} [E(ix) - E(-ix)]$$

Define $C(x) = \frac{1}{2} [E(ix)+ E(-ix)]$ and $S(x) = \frac{1}{2i} [E(ix) - E(-ix)]$. Prove that $C(x)$ and $S(x)$ are real for any real $x$.
$$\begin{align} E(x) &= \summ \frac{x^n}{n!} \\ &= 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^3}{3!} + \dots \\ E(ix) &= 1 + ix + \frac{(ix)^2}{2!} + \frac{(ix)^3}{3!} + \frac{(ix)^4}{4!} + \frac{(ix)^5}{5!} + \dots \\ E(-ix) &= 1 - ix + \frac{(-ix)^2}{2!} + \frac{(-ix)^3}{3!} + \frac{(-ix)^4}{4!} + \frac{(-ix)^5}{5!} + \dots\\ &= 1 - ix + \frac{(ix)^2}{2!} - \frac{(ix)^3}{3!} + \frac{(ix)^4}{4!} - \frac{(ix)^5}{5!} + \dots\\ C(x) &= \frac{1}{2} [E(ix) + E(-ix)]\\ &= \frac{1}{2}(2 + 2 \frac{(ix)^2}{2!} + 2 \frac{(ix)^4}{4!} + \dots )\\ &= 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \dots \\ S(x) &= \frac{1}{2i} [E(ix) - E(-ix)]\\ &= \frac{1}{2i}(2xi + 2 \frac{(ix)^3}{3!} + 2 \frac{(ix)^5}{5!} + \dots )\\ &= x + \frac{i^2 x^3}{3!} + \frac{i^4 x^5}{5!} + \dots \\ &= x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \dots \\ \end{align}$$ Thus $C(x)$ and $S(x)$ are real for any real $x$.
