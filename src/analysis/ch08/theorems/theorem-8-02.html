--- title: Theorem 8.2 parent: powerSeries08 order: 2 ---

Suppose $\sum c_n$ converges. Put

$$f(x) = \Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n x^n (-1 < x < 1)$$

Then $\Lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = \summ c_n$

Let $s = \summ c_n$, $s_n = c_0 + c_1 + \dots + c_n$ and $s_{-1} = 0$.

Then $c_n = s_n - s_{n-1}$.

$$\begin{align} \Sum_{n=0}^{m} c_n x^n &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} (s_n - s_{n-1}) x^n \\ &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_n x^n - \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_{n-1} x^n \\ &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_n x^n - \Sum_{n=1}^{m} s_{n-1} x^{n} (\because s_{-1} = 0) \\ &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_n x^n - x\Sum_{n=1}^{m} s_{n-1} x^{n-1} \\ &= s_m x^m + \Sum_{n=0}^{m-1} s_n x^n - x\Sum_{n=0}^{m-1} s_{n} x^{n} \\ &= s_m x^m + (1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{m-1} s_n x^n \\ \end{align}$$

For $\abs{x} < 1$, we let $m \to \infty$ and obtain $f(x)=(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} s_n x^n$

Also $(1-x)\summ x^n = (1-x) \frac{1}{1-x} = 1$

Let $ s = \summ c_n$. Since $\{s_n\}$ is the sequence of partial sums, we have $ s= \summ c_n = \limit s_n$.

Let $\ep > 0 $ be given. Since $s= \limit s_n$, there is an integer $N$ such that $n > N$ implies $\abs{s - s_n} < \frac{\ep}{2}$.

$$\begin{align*} \abs{f(x) - s} &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} s_n x^n - s (1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} s_n x^n - (1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} sx^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (s_n - s) x^n} \\ &\begin{array}{ll} \leq & \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n}\\ &+ \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} (s_n - s) x^n} \end{array} \tag{a} \end{align*}$$

We choose $M > 0$ such that $M > \abs{s_n - s}$ for $n=0,1,2,3, \dots N$ and $M > \frac{\ep}{2}$.

$$\begin{array}{rcl} 0 <& \frac{\ep}{2} &< M \\ 0 <& \frac{\ep}{2M} &< 1 \\ 0 >& -\frac{\ep}{2M} &> -1 \\ 1 >& 1-\frac{\ep}{2M} &> 0 \\ 0 <& 1-\frac{\ep}{2M} &< 1 \\ 0 <& \sqrt[N+1]{1-\frac{\ep}{2M}} &< 1 \end{array}$$

We choose $\delta = \sqrt[N+1]{1-\frac{\ep}{2M}}$. For $\delta < x < 1$,

$$\begin{align} \sqrt[N+1]{1-\frac{\ep}{2M}} &< x \\ 1-\frac{\ep}{2M} &< x^{N+1} \\ 1 - x^{N+1} &< \frac{\ep}{2M} \\ M(1 - x^{N+1}) &< \frac{\ep}{2} \\ \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} \abs{(1-x) \Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n-s) x^n} &< \abs{(1-x) \Sum_{n=0}^{N} M x^n}\\ &= \abs{(1-x) M \Sum_{n=0}^{N} x^n}\\ &= \abs{(1-x) M \frac{1(1-x^{N+1})}{1-x}}\\ &= \abs{M(1-x^{N+1})}\\ &< \frac{\ep}{2} \qquad{(b)} \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} (s_n - s) x^n} &\leq (1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \abs{s_n - s} x^n \\ &< (1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \frac{\ep}{2} x^n \\ &= (1-x) \frac{\ep}{2} \Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} x^n \\ &= (1-x) \frac{\ep}{2} \frac{x^{N+1}}{1-x} \\ &= \frac{\ep}{2} x^{N+1} \\ &< \frac{\ep}{2} \qquad{(c)} \end{align}$$ because $x^{N+1} < 1$ for $ -1 < x < 1$.

From (a), (b) and (c), we get $\abs{f(x) -s} < \ep$ if $\delta < x < 1$. The proof is completed.

Application of Theorem 8.2: Proving {% a 3.51 %}

If the series $\sum a_n, \sum b_n, \sum c_n$ converge to $A$, $B$, $C$, and $c_n = a_0 b_n + ... + a_n b_0$, then $C = AB$.

Let $f(x) =\sum a_n x^n$, $g(x) = \sum b_n x^n$ and $h(x) = \sum c_n x^n$ for $-1 < x < 1$.

For $\abs{x} < 1$, we consider $f(x)$.

$$\begin{align} \limit \sup \sqrt[n]{\abs{a_n x^n}} &= \limit \sup \sqrt[n]{\abs{a_n}} \abs{x} \\ &= \abs{x} \limit \sup \sqrt[n]{\abs{a_n}} \\ &< 1 (\because \abs{x} < 1 \text{ and } \{a_n\} \text{ converges.}) \end{align}$$

Thus the series converges absolutely. Similarly, the remaining series converge absolutely.

$$\begin{align} f(x)g(x) &= (\sum a_n x^n) (\sum b_n x^n) \\ &= (a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \dots ) (b_0 + b_1 x + b_2 x^2 + \dots) \\ &= \small{a_0 b_0 + (a_0 b_1 +a_1 b_0)x + (a_0 b_2 + a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_0) x^2 + \dots} \\ &= c_0 + c_1 x + c_2 x^2 + \dots \\ &= h(x) \end{align}$$

By Theorem 8.2, $f(x) \to A, g(x) \to B, h(x) \to C$ as $ x \to 1$.

$$\begin{align} \therefore \lim_{x \to 1} f(x)g(x) &= \lim_{x \to 1} h(x) \\ AB &= C \end{align}$$
