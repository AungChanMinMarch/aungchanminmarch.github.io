--- title: Theorem 8.2 Parent: powerSeries8 ---

Suppose $\sum c_n$ converges. Put

$$f(x) = \Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n x^n (-1 < x < 1)$$

Then $\Lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = \summ c_n$

Let $s_n = c_0 + c_1 + \dots + \cdots + c_n$ and $s_{-1} = 0$.

Then $c_n = s_n - s_{n-1}$

$$\begin{align} \Sum_{n=0}^{m} c_n x^n &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} (s_n - s_{n-1}) x^n \\ &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_n x^n - \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_{n-1} x^n \\ &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_n x^n - x\Sum_{n=1}^{m} s_{n-1} x^{n-1} (\because s_{-1} = 0) \\ &= \Sum_{n=0}^{m} s_n x^n - x\Sum_{n=0}^{m-1} s_{n} x^{n} \\ &= (1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{m-1} s_n x^n + s_m x^m \\ \end{align}$$

For $\abs{x} < 1$, we let $m \to \infty$ and obtain $f(x)=(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} s_n x^n$

Also $(1-x)\summ x^n = (1-x) \frac{1}{1-x} = 1$

Since $\sum c_n$ converges, $\{s_n \}$ also converges.

Suppose $s= \limit s_n$. Let $\ep > 0 $ be given. Then there is an integer $N$ such that $n > N$ implies $\abs{s - s_n} < \frac{\ep}{2}$.

$$\begin{align*} \abs{f(x) - s} &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} s_n x^n - s (1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} s_n x^n - (1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} sx^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (s_n - s) x^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n + (1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} (s_n - s) x^n} \\ &\leq \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n} + \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} (s_n - s) x^n} \\ &\leq \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n} + (1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \abs{s_n - s} \abs{x^n} \\ &< \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n} + (1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \frac{\ep}{2} \abs{x^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n} + \frac{\ep}{2} (1-x)\Sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \abs{x^n} \\ &= \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n} + \frac{\ep}{2} (1-x) \frac{x^{N+1}}{1-x} \\ &< \abs{(1-x)\Sum_{n=0}^{N} (s_n - s) x^n} + \frac{\ep}{2} (\because x^{N+1} < 1 \mbox{ for } \abs{x} < 1)\\ &\leq \ep \\ \therefore \limit f(x) &= s = \summ c_n \end{align*}$$
