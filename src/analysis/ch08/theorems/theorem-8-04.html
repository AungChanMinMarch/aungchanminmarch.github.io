--- title: 'Theorem 8.4' parent: 'powerSeries08' order: 4 ---
Suppose $$f(x) = \summ c_n x^n,$$ the series converging in $\abs{x} < R$. If $-R < a < R$, then $f$ can be expanded in a power series about the point $x = a$ which converges in $\abs{x - a} < R - \abs{a}$, and $$f(x) = \summ \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!} (x - a)^n (\abs{x-a} < R - \abs{a}).$$

This is an extension of Theorem 5.15 and is also known as Taylor's theorem.

$$\begin{align} f(x) &= \summ c_n x^n \\ &= \summ c_n [(x-a) + a]^n \\ &= \summ c_n \sum_{m=0}^{n} \binom{n}{m} a^{n-m} (x-a)^m\\ & \begin{array}{rl} =\summ c_n \{ \binom{n}{0} a^n (x-a)^0 +& \binom{n}{1} a^{n-1}(x-a) + \\ \binom{n}{2} a^{n-2} (x-a)^2 + & \dots \} \end{array} \\ &\begin{array}{ll} =& \binom{0}{0} c_0 a^0 (x-a)^0 + \\ & \binom{1}{0} c_1 a^1 (x-a)^0 + \binom{1}{1} c_1 a^0 (x-a) +\\ & \binom{2}{0} c_2 a^2 (x-a)^0 + \binom{2}{1} c_2 a(x-a) + \\ & \binom{2}{2} c_2 a^0 (x-a)^2 + \\ &\binom{3}{0} c_3 a^3 (x-a)^0 + \binom{3}{1} c_3 a^2 (x-a)^1 +\\ &\binom{3}{2} c_3 a^1 (x-a)^2 + \binom{3}{3} c_3 a^0 (x-a)^3 + \dots \end{array} \\ &\begin{array}{ll} =&\bracket{ \binom{0}{0} c_0 a^0 + \binom{1}{0} c_1 a^1 + \binom{2}{0} c_2 a^2 + \dots } (x-a)^0 +\\ &\bracket{ \binom{1}{1} c_1 a^0 + \binom{2}{1} c_2 a^1 + \binom{3}{1} c_2 a^2 + \dots } (x-a)^1 + \\ &\bracket{ \binom{2}{2} c_3 a^0 + \binom{3}{2} c_3 a^1 + \binom{4}{2} c_3 a^2 + \dots } (x-a)^2 + \\ &+\dots \end{array} \\ &\begin{array}{ll} =& \bracket{\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \binom{n}{0} c_n a^n} (x-a)^0 + \\ & \bracket{\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \binom{n}{1} c_n a^{n-1}} (x-a)^1 + \\ & \bracket{\sum_{n=2}^{\infty} \binom{n}{2} c_n a^{n-2}} (x-a)^2 +\\ & \dots \end{array} \\ &= \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sbracket{ \sum_{n=m} ^{\infty} \binom{n}{m} c_n a^{n-m} }(x-a)^m \end{align}$$

This is the desired expansion about the point $x=a$. Now we shall prove its validity. By {% a 8.3 %},

$$\begin{align} & \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sbracket{ \sum_{n=m} ^{\infty} \binom{n}{m} c_n a^{n-m} }(x-a)^m\\ =& \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sum_{n=m}^{\infty} \binom{n}{m} c_n a^{n-m}(x-a)^m\\ =& \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \sum_{m=0}^{n} \abs{c_n \binom{n}{m} a^{n-m} (x-a)^{m}}\\ =&\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \sum_{m=0}^{n} \abs{c_n} \binom{n}{m} \abs{a^{n-m}} \abs{(x-a)^{m}} \\ =&\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \abs{c_n} \sum_{m=0}^{n} \binom{n}{m} \abs{a}^{n-m} \abs{x-a}^m \\ =&\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \abs{c_n} \bracket{\abs{a} + \abs{x-a}}^n \end{align}$$

converges if $\abs{x-a} < R - \abs{a}$. Thus we have

$$\begin{align} &\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \sum_{m=0}^{n} c_n \binom{n}{m} a^{n-m} (x-a)^{m}\\ =& \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sbracket{ \sum_{n=m} ^{\infty} \binom{n}{m} c_n a^{n-m} }(x-a)^m \\ =& f(x) \end{align}$$

converges if $\abs{x-a} < R - \abs{a}$.

By Corollary of {% a 8.1 %}, we have

\begin{align} f^{(m)}(a) &= \sum\limits_{n=m}^{\infty} n(n-1) \cdots (n-m+1) c_n a^{n-m} \\ &= \sum\limits_{n=m}^{\infty} n(n-1) \cdots (n-m+1) \frac{(n-m)!}{(n-m)!} c_n a^{n-m} \\ &= \sum\limits_{n=m}^{\infty} \frac{n!}{(n-m)!} c_n a^{n-m} \\ \frac{f^{(m)}(a)}{m!} &= \sum\limits_{n=m}^{\infty} \frac{n!}{(n-m)! m!} c_n a^{n-m} \\ &= \sum\limits_{n=m}^{\infty} \binom{n}{m} c_n a^{n-m} \\ &\text{For } \abs{x-a} < R- \abs{a}, \\ f(x) &= \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sbracket{ \sum_{n=m} ^{\infty} \binom{n}{m} c_n a^{n-m} }(x-a)^m \\ &= \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sbracket{\frac{f^{(m)}(a)}{m!}}(x-a)^m \\ &= \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x-a)^n \\ \end{align}
