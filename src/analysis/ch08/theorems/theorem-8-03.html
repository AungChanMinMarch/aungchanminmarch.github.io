--- title: Theorem 8.3 parent: powerSeries08 order: 3 ---
Given a double sequence $\{a_{ij}\}, i=1,2,3,\cdots, j=1,2,3,\dots$ suppose that $$\sum\limits_{j=1}^{\infty} \abs{a_ij} = b_i, i=1,2,3,\dots$$ and $\sum b_i$ converges. Then $$\Sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \Sum_{j=1}^{\infty} a_{ij} = \Sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{\infty} a_{ij}$$
Let $E=\{x_0, x_1, x_2, \dots \}$ and suppose $x_n \to x_0$ as $n \to \infty$. Define $$\begin{align} f_{i} (x_n) &= \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} \\ f_i (x_0) &= \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} a_{ij} \\ \end{align}$$

Then $\abs{f_i(x)} \leq \Sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \abs{a_{ij}} = b_i$. Hence, by {% a '7.10' %}, $\sum\limits_{i=1}^{\infty} f_i(x)$ converges uniformly.

Let $g_m(x) = \Sum_{i=1}^{m} f_i (x)$.

Since the series $\Sum_{i=1}^{\infty} f_i(x)$ converges uniformly, the sequence of its partial sums $\{ g_m(x) \}$ converges uniformly. Hence by {% a 7.11 %},

$$\begin{align} \lim_{m \to \infty} \lim_{t \to x} g_m(t) &=\lim_{t \to x} \lim_{m \to \infty} g_m(t) \\ \lim_{m \to \infty} \lim_{n \to \infty} g_m(x_n) &=\lim_{n \to \infty} \lim_{m \to \infty} g_m(x_n) \\ \lim_{m \to \infty} \lim_{n \to \infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{m} f_i(x_n) &=\lim_{n \to \infty} \lim_{m \to \infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{m} f_i(x_n) \\ \lim_{m \to \infty} \lim_{n \to \infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{m} \Sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} &=\lim_{n \to \infty} \lim_{m \to \infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{m} \Sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} \\ \lim_{m \to \infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{m} \lim_{n \to \infty} \Sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} &=\lim_{n \to \infty} \Sum_{j=1}^{n} \lim_{m \to \infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{m} a_{ij} \\ \Sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \Sum_{j=1}^{\infty} a_{ij} &= \Sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \Sum_{i=1}^{\infty} a_{ij} \end{align}$$
