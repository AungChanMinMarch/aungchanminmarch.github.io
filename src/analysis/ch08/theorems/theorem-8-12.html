--- title: "Theorem 8.12" order: 12 parent: fourierSeries08 ---

If $\{\phi_n\}$ is orthonormal on $[a, b]$, and if $f(x) \sim \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} c_n \phi_n(x)$, then $$ \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \abs{c_n}^2 \leq \int\limits_a^b \abs{f(x)}^2 dx \tag{73}$$ In particular, $$\limit c_n =0 \tag{74}$$

Let $s_n = \sum\limits_{m=1}^{n} c_m \phi_m(x) dx$.

Then, in {% a 7.11 %}, we have seen $$\int\limits_a^b \abs{f(x) -s_n(x)} dx = \int\limits_{a}^{b} \abs{f(x)}^2 dx - \sum\limits_{m=1}^{n} \abs{c_m}^2$$

{% include "./shared_8-11_8-12.liquid" %}
$$\begin{align} \int\limits_a^b \abs{f(x) -s_n(x)} dx &\geq 0 \\ \int\limits_{a}^{b} \abs{f(x)}^2 dx - \sum\limits_{m=1}^{n} \abs{c_m}^2 &\geq 0 \\ \int\limits_{a}^{b} \abs{f(x)}^2 dx &\geq \sum\limits_{m=1}^{n} \abs{c_m}^2 \\ \end{align}$$

Letting $n \to \infty$, we get

$$ \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \abs{c_n}^2 \leq \int\limits_a^b \abs{f(x)}^2 dx$$

By {% a 3.23 %}, we get

$$\limit c_n =0$$
