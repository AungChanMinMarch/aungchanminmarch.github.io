--- title: Theorem 8.1 parent: powerSeries8 ---

Suppose the series $$\summ c_n x^n \tag{3}$$ converges for $\abs{x} < R$, and define $f(x)=\summ c_n x^n$ ($\abs{x} < R$).

Then (3) converges uniformly on $[-R+\ep, R- \ep ]$, no matter which $\ep > 0$ is chosen. The function $f$ is continuous and differentiable in $(-R,R)$ and $f'(x) = \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} n c_n x^{n-1} (\abs{x} < R)$

Let $\ep > 0$ be given.

$$\begin{align} \text{If } \abs{x} &\leq R - \ep, \\ \abs{x}^n &\leq \abs{R - \ep}^n \\ \abs{c_n} \abs{x^n} &\leq \abs{c_n} \abs{R - \ep}^n \\ \abs{c_n x^n} &\leq \abs{c_n (R - \ep)^n} \end{align}$$

$$\begin{align} \limit \sup \abs{c_n(R-\ep)^n}^{\frac{1}{n}} &= \limit \sup ( \abs{c_n}^{\farc{1}{n}} (R-\ep)) \\ &= \alpha (R - \ep)\\ &= \frac{1}{R} (R-\ep)\\ &= 1 - \frac{\ep}{R} \end{align}$$

Since $\abs{R- \ep} < R$ and $\summ c_n x^n$ converges for $\abs{x} < R$, $\summ c_n (R - \ep)^n$ converges.

Hence, by {% a '7.10' %}, $\summ \abs{c_n x^n}$ converges uniformly on $[-R+\ep, R- \ep ]$
