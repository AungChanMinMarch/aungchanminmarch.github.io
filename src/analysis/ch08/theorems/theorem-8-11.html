Let $\{\phi_n\}$ be orthonormal on $[a, b]$. Let $$s_n(x) = \sum_{m=1}^{n} c_m\phi_m(x) \tag{68}$$ be the nth partial sum of the Fourier series of $f$, and suppose $$t_n(x) = \sum_{m=1}^{n} \gamma_m \phi(x) \tag{69}$$ Then $$\int\limits_{a}^{b} \abs{f-s_n}^2 dx \leq \int\limits_{a}^{b} \abs{f-t_n}^2 dx \tag{70}$$ and equality holds if and only if $$\gamma_m = c_m (m=1,2,3,\cdots, n) \tag{71}$$

Now we shall do the same process for $\int \abs{f-t_n}^2 dx$

$$\begin{align} \int \abs{f-t_n}^2 &= \int (f-t_n) \conj{(f-t_n)} \\ &= \int (f- t_n)(\conj{f} - \conj{t_n}) \\ &= \int \abs{f}^2 - \int f \conj{t_n} - \int \conj{f} t_n + \int \abs{t_n}^2 \tag{b} \end{align}$$

Now we need to find $\int f \conj{t_n}$, $\int \conj{f} t_n$ and $\int \abs{t_n}^2$.

$$\begin{align} \int f \conj{t_n} =& \int f \conj{\sum \gamma_m \phi_m} \\ =& \int f \bracket{\conj{\gamma_1} \conj{\phi_1} + \dots + \conj{\gamma_n} \conj{\phi_n}} \\ =& \int f \conj{\gamma_1} \conj{\phi_m} + \dots + \int f \conj{\gamma_n} \conj{\phi_n} \\ =& \conj{\gamma_1} \int f \conj{\phi_m} + \dots + \conj{\gamma_n} \int f \conj{\phi_n} \\ =& \conj{\gamma_1} c_1 + \cdots + \conj{\gamma_n} c_n \\ =& \sum \conj{\gamma_m} c_m \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} \int \conj{f} t_n =& \int \conj{f} \sum \gamma_m \phi_m \\ =& \int \conj{f} \bracket{\gamma_1 \phi_1 + \cdots + \gamma_n \phi_n} \\ =& \int \gamma_1 \conj{f} \phi_1 dx + \cdots + \int \gamma_n \conj{f} \phi_n \\ =& \gamma_1 \int \conj{f} \phi_1 + \cdots + \gamma_n \int \conj{f} \phi_n \\ =& \gamma_1 \conj{c_1} + \cdots + \gamma_n \conj{c_n} \\ =& \sum \gamma_m \conj{c_m} \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align} \int \abs{t_n}^2 &= \int t_n \conj{t_n} \\ &= \int \sum \gamma_m \phi_m \sum \conj{\gamma_m} \conj{\phi_m} \\ &= \int \bracket{\gamma_1 \phi_1 + \cdots + \gamma_n \phi_n} \bracket{\conj{\gamma_1} \conj{\phi_1} + \dots + \conj{\gamma_n} \conj{\phi_n}} \\ &= \gamma_1 \conj{\gamma_1} + \cdots + \gamma_n \conj{\gamma_n} \\ &= \sum \abs{\gamma_m}^2 \end{align}$$ Hence From(b), we get $$\begin{align} \int \abs{f-t_n}^2 &= \int \abs{f}^2 - \int f \conj{\gamma_n} - \int \conj{f} \gamma_n + \int \abs{\gamma_n}^2 \\ &= \int \abs{f}^2 - \sum \conj{\gamma_m} c_m - \sum \gamma_m \conj{c_m} + \sum \abs{\gamma_m}^2 \\ &= \begin{array}{l} \int \abs{f}^2 - \sum \abs{c_m}^2 + \\ \sum \abs{c_m}^2 - \sum \conj{\gamma_m} c_m - \sum \gamma_m \conj{c_m} + \sum \abs{\gamma_m}^2 \\ \end{array} \\ %&= \int \abs{f}^2 - \sum \abs{c_m}^2 + \sum \bracket{c_m(\conj{c_m} - \conj{\gamma_m}) - \gamma_m(\conj{c_m} - \conj{\gamma_m}} \\ &= \int \abs{f}^2 - \sum \abs{c_m}^2 + \sum (c_m - \gamma)(\conj{c_m} - \conj{\gamma_m}) \\ &= \int \abs{f}^2 - \sum \abs{c_m}^2 + \sum \abs{c_m - \gamma_m}^2 \end{align}$$ $$\therefore \sum \abs{c_m - \gamma_m}^2 = \int \abs{f-t_n}^2 dx - \int \abs{f-s_n}^2 dx$$
