Prove the following limit relations

(a) Prove $\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{b^x -1}{x} = \log b$ where $b > 0$.
$$\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{b^x -1}{x} (\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0}) $$ $$\begin{align} \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{b^x -1}{x} &= \lim_{x \to 0} b^x \log b \\ &= b^0 \log b\\ &= \log b \end{align}$$
(b) Prove $\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\log (1+x)}{x} = 1$.
$$\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\log(1+x)}{x} (\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0})$$ $$\begin{align} \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\log(1+x)}{x} &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{1+x} \\ &= \frac{1}{1+0}\\ &= 1 \end{align}$$
(c) Prove $\lim\limits_{x \to 0} (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}} = e$.
$$\begin{align} \lim\limits_{x \to 0} (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}} &= \lim_{x \to 0} e^{\log (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}}} \\ &= e^{\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\log (1+x)}{x}} \bracket{\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0}}\\ &= e^{\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{1+x}} \\ &= e^{\frac{1}{1+0}} \\ &= e \end{align}$$
(d) Prove $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty} \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}^n = e^x$.
$$\begin{align} \lim\limits_{n \to \infty} \log \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}^n &= \limit n \log \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}\\ &= \limit \frac{\log \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}}{\frac{1}{n}} \\ &\bracket{\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0}}\\ &= \limit \frac{\frac{1}{1+\frac{x}{n}} (-\frac{x}{n^2})}{-\frac{1}{n^2}} \\ &= \limit x \frac{1}{1 + \frac{x}{n}} \\ &= x \frac{1}{1+0} \\ &= x\\ \therefore \limit \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}^n &= \limit e^{\log \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}^n} \\ &= e^{\lim\limits_{n \to \infty} \log \bracket{1+\frac{x}{n}}^n}\\ &= e^x \end{align}$$
