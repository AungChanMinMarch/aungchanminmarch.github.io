For $n=0,1,2, \dots,$ and $x$ real, prove that $$ \abs{\sin nx} \leq n \abs{\sin x}$$ Note that this inequility may be false for other values of $x$. For instance, $\abs{\sin \frac{1}{2}\pi} > \frac{1}{2}\abs{\sin x}$

We shall prove $\abs{\sin nx} \leq n \abs{\sin x}$ for $n=0,1,2,\dots$ by induction.

It is obvious that the statement is true for $n=0$ and $n=1$.

Suppose that the statement is true for $n=k$, i.e. $\abs{\sin kx} \leq k \abs{\sin x}$.

$$\begin{align} \abs{\sin ((k+1)x)} &= \abs{ \sin (kx + x) } \\ &= \abs{\sin kx \cos x + \cos kx \sin x} \\ &\leq \abs{\sin kx \cos x} + \abs{\cos kx \sin x} \\ &= \abs{\sin kx} \abs{\cos x} + \abs{\cos kx} \abs{\sin x} \\ &\leq \abs{\sin kx} + \abs{\sin x} \\ &\leq k \abs{\sin x} + \abs{\sin x} \\ &= (k+1) \abs{\sin x}\\ \therefore \abs{\sin (k+1)x} &\leq (k+1) \abs{\sin x}\\ \end{align}$$

The statement is true for $n=k+1$. Hence by mathematical induction, the statement is true for $n=0,1,2, \dots$.
