Find the following limits

(a) $\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{e - (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}}}{x}$
$$\begin{align} \lim_{x \to 0} \log (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}} &= \lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\log (1+x)}{x} \\ &\bracket{\text{Indeterminate Form: } \frac{0}{0}}\\ &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{1+x} \\ &= \frac{1}{1+0} \\ &= 1 \\ \therefore \lim_{x \to 0} (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}} &= \lim_{x \to 0} e^{\log (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}}} \\ &= e \\ \end{align}$$ \begin{align} &\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{e - (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}}}{x}\\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e - e^{\log (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}}}}{x} \bracket{\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0}} \\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} -e^{\log (1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}}} \bracket{\frac{1}{x} \frac{1}{1+x} - \frac{1}{x^2} \log (1+x)} \\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} -(1+x)^{\frac{1}{x}} \bracket{\frac{x-(1+x)\log(1+x)}{x^2(1+x)}}\\ =& -e \bracket{ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x - \log(1+x) - x \log(1+x)}{x^2 + x^3}}\\ &\bracket{\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0}} \\ =& -e \bracket{ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \frac{1}{1+x} - \log(1+x) - \frac{x}{1+x}}{2x + 3x^2}} \\ =& -e \bracket{ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{- \log(1+x)}{2x + 3x^2}} \\ &\bracket{\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{0}{0}} \\ =& -e \bracket{ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{-1}{1+x}}{2+6x}} \\ =& -e \bracket{\frac{-1}{2}} \\ = &\frac{e}{2} \end{align}$$
(b) $\limit \frac{n}{\log n} [n^{\frac{1}{n}} - 1]$
$$\begin{align} \limit n^{\frac{1}{n}} &= \limit e^{\log n^{\frac{1}{n}}} \\ &= \limit e^{\frac{\log n}{n}} \bracket{\text{indeterminate form: } \frac{\infty}{\infty}} \\ &= \limit e^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ &= e^0 \\ &= 1 \end{align}$$ Also $\limit \frac{\log n}{n} = \limit \frac{1}{n} = 0$ $$\begin{align} \limit \frac{n}{\log n} [n^{\frac{1}{n}} - 1] &= \limit \frac{n^{\frac{1}{n}} - 1}{\frac{\log n}{n}} \\ &\indeterminatezero \\ &= \limit \frac{\frac{d}{dn} e^{\log n^{\frac{1}{n}}}}{\frac{d}{dn} \frac{\log n}{n} } \\ &= \limit \frac{n^{\frac{1}{n}} \frac{d}{dn} \dfrac{\log n}{n} }{\frac{d}{dn} \dfrac{\log n}{n} }\\ &= \limit n^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ &= 1 \end{align}$$
(c) $\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\tan x -x}{x(1- \cos x)}$
$$\begin{align} &\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x -x}{x(1- \cos x)} \indeterminatezero \\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sec^2 x -1}{1-\cos x + x \sin x} \indeterminatezero \\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \sec^2 x \tan x}{\sin x + x \cos x + \sin x} \\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \sec^2 x \tan x}{2\sin x + x \cos x} \indeterminatezero\\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4 \sec^2 x \tan^2 x + 2 \sec^4 x}{2\cos x - x \sin x + \cos x} \\ =& \frac{0 + 2}{2 - 0 + 1} \\ =& \frac{2}{3} \end{align}$$
(d) $\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \dfrac{x - \sin x}{\tan x - x}$
$$\begin{align} &\lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{x - \sin x}{\tan x - x} \indeterminatezero\\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{\sec^2 x - 1} \indeterminatezero \\ =& \lim{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{2 \sec^2 x\tan x} \indeterminatezero \\ =& \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\cos x}{4 \sec^2 x \tan^2 x + 2 \sec^4 x} \\ =& \frac{1}{0 + 2} \\ =& \frac{1}{2} \end{align}$$
