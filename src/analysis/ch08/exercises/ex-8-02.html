Let $a_{ij}$ be the number in the $i^{th}$ row and $j^{th}$ column of the array

$$\begin{array} -1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots \\ \frac{1}{2} & -1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots \\ \frac{1}{4} & \frac{1}{2} & -1 & 0 & \cdots \\ \frac{1}{8} & \frac{1}{4} & \frac{1}{2} & -1 & \cdots \\ \cdots & \cdots & \cdots & \cdots & \cdots \end{array}$$

so that

$$ a_{ij} = \begin{cases} 0 &\mbox{(i < j)}\\ -1 &\mbox{(i=j)} \\ 2^{j-i} &\mbox{(i > j)} \end{cases}$$

Prove that $\sum\limits_i \sum\limits_j a_{ij} = -2$ and $\sum\limits_{j} \sum\limits_i a_{ij} = 0$

First, fix $i$.

$$\begin{align*} \Sum_{j=0}^{\infty} a_{ij} &= \Sum_{j=0}^{i-1} a_{ij} + a_{ii} + \Sum_{j=i+1}^{\infty} a_{ij} \\ &= \Sum_{j=0}^{i-1} 2^{j-i} + (-1) + \Sum_{j=i+1}^{\infty} 0 \\ &= \frac{2^{-i}(2^i - 1)}{2 - 1} -1 \\ &= 1 - 2^{-i} -1\\ &= - (\frac{1}{2})^i \\ \Sum_{i=0}^{\infty} \Sum_{j=0}^{\infty} a_{ij} &= \Sum_{i=0}^{\infty} -(\frac{1}{2})^i \\ &= - \frac{1}{1-\frac{1}{2}} \\ &= -2 \end{align*}$$ $$\begin{align*} \end{align*}$$

Now, fix $j$.

$$\begin{align*} \Sum_{i=0}^{\infty} a_{ij} &= \Sum_{i=0}^{j-1} a_{ij} + a_{jj} + \Sum_{i=j+1}^{\infty} a_{ij} \\ &= \Sum_{i=0}^{j-1} 0 + (-1) + \Sum_{i=j+1}^{\infty} 2^{j-i} \\ &= -1 + \frac{2^{j-(j+1)}}{1-\frac{1}{2}} \\ &= -1 + 1 \\ &= 0 \\ \Sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \Sum_{i=0}^{\infty} a_{ij} &= \Sum_{j=0}^{\infty} 0 = 0 \end{align*}$$
