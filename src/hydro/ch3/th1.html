Theroem 1 (page 104)

If $S$ is the boundary of a spherical surface lying wholly within the fluid, then the mean value of the velocity potential is equal to its value at the centre of the sphere.

Let $r$ be the radius of $S$. Then the surface area of the sphere is $4 \pi r^2$

Let $\bar{\phi}$ be the mean value of the velocity potential $\varphi$ on $S$. Then $$\begin{align*} \bar{\phi} &= \frac{1}{4 \pi r^2} \int_S \varphi dS \\ \partialwrt[\bar{\phi}]{r} &= \frac{1}{4 \pi r^2} \int_S \partialwrt[\varphi]{r} dS \\ &= \frac{1}{4 \pi r^2} \int_S \partialwrt[\varphi]{n} dS \\ &= \frac{1}{4 \pi r^2} \int_S \hat{n}.\grad \varphi dS \\ &= \frac{1}{4 \pi r^2} \int_V \grad.\grad \varphi dv \\ &=0 (\because \grad^2 \varphi = 0 \mbox{ in the interiror of $S$}) \end{align*}$$

Hence $\bar{\phi}$ is independent of $r$. Therefore the mean value of velocity potential is equal to its value at the centre of the sphere.
