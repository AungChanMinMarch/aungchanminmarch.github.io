Three liquids, whose densities are in A.P., fill a semi- circular tube whose bounding diameter is horizontal. Prove that the depth of one of the common surfaces is double that of the other.

(M.T. 1861; Calcutta 1916; Patna 1927; Rangoon 1937; Utkal 1948)

Let $P$ and $Q$ be the common surfaces of the liquids.

Let the densities of liquids be $\rho-d, \rho, \rho+d$.

Let $BQ$, $QP$ and $CP$ contain the liquid of density $\rho - d$, $\rho +d$ and $\rho$ respectively.

Let $A$ be the lowest point of the tube.

The pressures must be the same on the two sides of $A$.

$$\begin{align*} (\rho - d)gOQ' + (\rho + d)gQ'A &= \rho g OP' + (\rho + d)gP'A \\ (\rho - d)OQ' + (\rho + d)Q'A &= \rho OP' + (\rho + d)P'A \\ (\rho - d)OQ' + (\rho + d)(OA - OQ') &= \rho OP' + (\rho + d)(OA-OP') \\ (\rho - d)OQ' + (\rho + d)OA - (\rho +d) OQ' &= \rho OP' + (\rho + d)OA-(\rho +d)OP' \\ (\rho - d)OQ' - (\rho +d) OQ' &= \rho OP' -(\rho +d)OP' \\ \rho OQ' - d OQ' - \rho OQ' - d OQ' &= \rho OP' - \rho OP' - dOP' \\ -2 d OQ' &= - dOP' \\ OP' &= 2OQ' \end{align*}$$
