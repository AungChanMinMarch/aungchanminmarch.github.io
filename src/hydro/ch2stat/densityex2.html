Show that the specific gravity of a mixture of n liquids is greater when equal volumes are taken than when equal weights are taken, assuming no change in volume as the result of mixing. (Utka! 1946). Lets be the sp. gr. of the mixture when the mixture of equal volumes is taken and s' be that when the mixture of equal weights is taken.

Let $s_1, s_2, \dots, s_n$ be the specific gravity of $n$ liquids.

Let $s$ be the specific gravity of a mixture of n liquids is greater when equal volumes, $V$ are taken.

$$s=\dfrac{\Sum_{i=1}^n Vs_i}{\Sum_{i=1}^{n} V} = \dfrac{V\Sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i}{nV}= \frac{1}{n}(s_1+ s_2+ \dots + s_n)$$

Since Arithmetic Mean of $n$ unequal positive numbers is always geather than their Geometric Mean,

$$\begin{align*} \frac{s_1+ s_2+ \dots + s_n}{n} &> (s_1 \times s_2 \times \dots \times s_n)^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ s &> (s_1 s_2 \dots s_n)^{\frac{1}{n}} \end{align*}$$

Let $s'$ be the spectfic gravity of a mixture of n liquids is greater when equal weights, $W$ are taken.

$$\begin{align*} s'& = \frac{\Sum_{i=1}^{n} W}{\Sum_{i=1}^n \frac{W}{s_i}} = \frac{nW}{W\Sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{s_i}} = \frac{n}{\Sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{s_i}} \\ \frac{1}{s'} &= \frac{\Sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{s_i}}{n} = \frac{1}{n} \bracket{\frac{1}{s_1} + \frac{1}{s_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{s_n}} \end{align*}$$

Since Arithmetic Mean of $n$ unequal positive numbers is always geather than their Geometric Mean,

$$\begin{align*} \frac{1}{n} \bracket{\frac{1}{s_1} + \frac{1}{s_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{s_n}} &> \bracket{\frac{1}{s_1} \times \frac{1}{s_2} \times \dots \times \frac{1}{s_n}}^{\frac{1}{n}} = \bracket{\frac{1}{s_1 s_2 \dots s_n}}^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ \frac{1}{s'} &> \bracket{\frac{1}{s_1 s_2 \dots s_n}}^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ s' &< (s_1 s_2 \dots s_n)^{\frac{1}{n}} < s \\ \therefore s' &< s\\ \end{align*}$$
