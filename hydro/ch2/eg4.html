Example 4 (page 83)

Liquid flows through a pipe whose surface is the surface of revolution of the curve $y=a+\dfrac{kx^2}{a}$ about the $x$ x-axis ($-a \leq x \leq a$). If the liquid enters at the end $x=-a$ of the pipe with velocity $V$, show that the time taken by a liquid particle to traverse the entire length of the pipe from $x=-a$ to $x=+a$ is $$ \cbracket{\dfrac{2a}{V(1+k)^2}} \bracket{1+\dfrac{2}{3}k+\dfrac{1}{5}k^2}$$

At the section $x=-a$,
the velocity of the liquid is $V$
the radius of the section is $y=a+ \dfrac{k(-a)^2}{a} =a+ka=a(1+k)$
the area of the section is $\pi r^2 = \pi a^2(1+k)^2$

At the section distant $x$ from $0$,
let $\dfrac{dx}{dt}$ be the velocity of the liquid.
the radius of the section is $y=a+ \dfrac{kx^2}{a}$
the area of the section is $\pi r^2 = \pi \cbracket{a+ \dfrac{kx^2}{a}}^2 = \pi \cbracket{a\bracket{1+\dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}}^2 = \pi a^2 \bracket{1+\dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}^2$

Thus the equation of continuity is

$$\begin{align*} V \pi a^2 (1+k)^2 &= \dfrac{dx}{dt} \pi a^2 \bracket{1+\dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}^2\\ V (1+k)^2 &= \dfrac{dx}{dt} \bracket{1+\dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}^2 \\ dt &= \dfrac{1}{V(1+k)^2} \bracket{1+ \dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}^2 dx\\ t &=\int_{-a}^{a} \dfrac{1}{V(1+k)^2} \bracket{1+ \dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}^2 dx\\ &= \dfrac{2}{V(1+k)^2} \int_{0}^{a} \bracket{1+ \dfrac{kx^2}{a^2}}^2 dx\\ &= \dfrac{2}{V(1+k)^2} \int_{0}^{a} \bracket{1+ \dfrac{2kx^2}{a^2} + \dfrac{k^2x^4}{a^4}} dx\\ &= \dfrac{2}{V(1+k)^2} \sbracket{x+ \dfrac{2kx^3}{3a^2} + \dfrac{k^2x^5}{5a^4}}_{0}^{a}\\ &= \dfrac{2}{V(1+k)^2} \bracket{a+ \dfrac{2ka}{3} + \dfrac{k^2a}{5}}\\ &= \dfrac{2a}{V(1+k)^2} \bracket{1+ \dfrac{2}{3}k + \dfrac{1}{5}k^2}\\ \end{align*}$$
