Example 3 (page 82)
For a fluid moving in a fine tube of variable section $A$, prove from first principles that the equation of continuity is $$A \partialwrt[\rho]{t} + \partialwrt{s}(A \rho v) = 0$$ where $v$ is the speed at a point $P$ of the fluid and $s$ the length of the tube up to $P$.

What does this become for steady incompressible flow?

$v$ is the speed of the fluid at $P$.

Let $\rho$ is the density of the fluid at $t$.

Let $A$ be the area of the section at $P$.

Let $OPP'$ be the central streamline of the tube

let $s,s+\delta s$ be the arc lengths $OP, OP'$

At the section $A$ through $P$, the mass flux per unit time in the direction of $s$ increasing is $\dfrac{\rho V}{t} = \dfrac{\rho sA}{t} =\rho v A = A \rho v$ ($V$ is the volume).

At the neighbouring section $A+\delta A$ through $P'$, the mass flux per unit time in the direction of $s$ increasing is $A\rho v+ \delta s \dpartialwrt{s}(A\rho v)$.

Thus the net flow of mass into the elements between the sections $A$ and $A+\delta A$ is $-\delta s \dpartialwrt{s}(A\rho v)$

But the mass between the sections is $\rho A \delta s$

The rate of mass increasing is $\dpartialwrt{t} \rho A \delta s = A \delta s \dpartialwrt[\rho]{t}$

In the absence of sources and sinks, we have $-\delta s \partialwrt{s}(A\rho v)= A \delta s \partialwrt[\rho]{t}$

$$\therefore A \partialwrt[\rho]{t} + \partialwrt{s}(A\rho v)=0$$

For steady incompressible flow, $\rho$ is constant.

$$\begin{align*} 0+\rho \partialwrt{t}(vA) &= 0 \\ \partialwrt{t}(vA) &= 0 \\ vA&= \text{constant} \end{align*}$$
