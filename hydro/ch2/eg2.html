Example 2 (page 81)
For an incompressible fluid, $\vec{q} = [-wy, wx, 0]$ ($w=$constant). Discuss the nature of the flow.
$$\vec{q} = -wy \hat{i} + wx \hat{j} (k= \text{constant})$$

Since $\nabla \cdot \vec{q} = \dpartialwrt{x} (-wy) + \dpartialwrt{y} wx = 0$, the flow is a possible motion.

The equations of the streamlines are given by $$\dfrac{dx}{u} = \dfrac{dy}{v} = \dfrac{dz}{0}$$ $$\dfrac{dx}{-wy} = \dfrac{dy}{wx} = \dfrac{dz}{0}$$

$$\begin{align*} dz &= 0 \\ z &= \text{constant}\\ \frac{dx}{-wy} &= \frac{dy}{wx} \\ x dx &= -y dy \\ \frac{x^2}{2} &= - \frac{y^2}{2} + \text{constant}\\ \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{y^2}{2} &= \text{constant}\\ x^2 + y^2 &= \text{constant} \\ \end{align*}$$

The equations of the streamlines are $$\begin{align*} z &= \text{constant}\\ x^2 + y^2 &= \text{constant} \end{align*}$$

$$ \curl \vec{q} = \crossProduct{ \dpartialwrt{x} & \dpartialwrt{y} & \dpartialwrt{z} \\ -wy & wx & 0 } = 0 \hat{i} - 0\hat{j} + \bracket{\partialwrt{x}wx - \partialwrt{y}(-wy)} \hat{k} = 2w \hat{k} $$ Hence the flow is not the potential kind.
