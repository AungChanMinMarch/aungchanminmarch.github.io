Worked Example (page 97)
$AB$ is a tube of small uniform bore forming a quadrantal arc of a circle of radius $a$ and centre $O$, $OA$ being horizontal and $OB$ vertical with $B$ below $O$. The tube is full of liquid of density $\rho$, the end $B$ being closed. If $B$ is suddenly opened, show that initially $\dfrac{du}{dt} = \dfrac{2g}{\pi}$, where $u=u(t)$ is the velocity, and that the pressure at a point whose angular distance from $A$ is $\theta$ immediately drops to $\rho ga\bracket{\sin \theta - \dfrac{2\theta}{\pi}}$ above atomospheric pressure. Prove further that when the liquid remaining in the tube subtends an angle $\beta$ at the centre, $\dfrac{d^2 \beta}{dt^2} = -\dfrac{2g}{a \beta} \sin^2 \bracket{\frac{\beta}{2}}$

Since $\dfrac{d\vec{q}}{dt} = F - \frac{1}{\rho} \grad p$, the equation of motion of the element $PP'$ along the arc in the direction of $s$ increasing is $$\frac{du}{dt} = g \cos \theta - \frac{1}{\rho} \partialwrt[p]{s}$$ $$\frac{du}{dt} = \partialwrt[u]{t} \partialwrt[t]{t} + \partialwrt[u]{s} \partialwrt[s]{t} = \partialwrt[u]{t} + \partialwrt[u]{s} u = \partialwrt[u]{t} + \partialwrt{s}\bracket{\frac{1}{2} u^2}$$ $$\cos \theta = \frac{dy}{ds}$$ $$\partialwrt[u]{t} + \partialwrt{s}\bracket{\frac{1}{2} u^2}= g \frac{dy}{ds} - \frac{1}{\rho} \partialwrt[p]{s} \tag{1}$$ Integrating w.r.t. $s$, $$ s\partialwrt[u]{t} + \bracket{\frac{1}{2} u^2}= gy - \frac{p}{\rho} + f(t) \tag{2}$$

If $t=0$, then $u=0$.
When $t=0$ at $A$, we have $s=0, u=0, y=0, p=\Pi$. Hence Eq(2) gives $$0 = -\frac{\Pi}{\rho} + f(0) \tag{3}$$ When $t=0$ at $B$, we have $s=\dfrac{1}{2}\pi a, u=0, y=a, p = \Pi$. Hence Eq(2) gives $$\frac{1}{2} \pi a \partialwrt[u]{t} = ga - \frac{\Pi}{\rho} +f(0) \tag{4}$$ Equation (4) - (3) imples that $$\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2} \pi a \bracket{\partialwrt[u]{t}}_{t=0} &= ga \\ \bracket{\partialwrt[u]{t}}_{t=0} &= \frac{2g}{\pi} \\ \mbox{Hence } \bracket{\frac{du}{dt}}_{t=0} &= \bracket{\partialwrt[u]{t} + \partialwrt[u]{s} \partialwrt[s]{t}}_{t=0} \\ &= \bracket{\partialwrt[u]{t} + u \partialwrt[u]{s}}_{t=0}\\ &= \frac{2g}{\pi} (\because u=0 \text{ at } t=0) \end{align*}$$ When $t=0$ at a point whose angular distance from $A$ is $\theta$, we have $s=a \theta, u=0, y=a \sin \theta, \dpartialwrt[u]{t} = \dfrac{2g}{\pi}$. Hence Eq(2) gives $$a \theta \frac{2g}{\pi} = ga\sin \theta - \frac{p}{\rho} + f(0)$$ Using Eq(3), we get $f(0)=\frac{\Pi}{\rho}$. Hence $$\begin{align*} ga \frac{2\theta}{\pi} &= ga\sin \theta - \frac{p}{\rho} + \frac{\Pi}{\rho}\\ \rho ga \frac{2 \theta}{\pi} &= \rho ga\sin \theta - p + \Pi\\ p - \Pi &= \rho ga\sin \theta - \rho ga \frac{2\theta}{\pi}\\ &= \rho ga \bracket{\sin \theta - \frac{2\theta}{\pi}}\\ \end{align*}$$ Hence the pressure immediately drops to $\rho ga \bracket{\sin \theta - \dfrac{2\theta}{\pi}}$.

When the liquid remaining in the tube subtends an angle $\beta$ at the centre, at the upper surface, we have $s=a(\dfrac{\pi}{2} - \beta), y = a \cos \beta, p =\Pi$. Hence Eq(2) gives $$ \begin{align*} a(\frac{\pi}{2}-\beta)\partialwrt[u]{t} + \bracket{\frac{1}{2} u^2} &= g a \cos \beta - \frac{\Pi}{\rho} + f(t) \\ \frac{1}{2} \pi a \partialwrt[u]{t} - a\beta\partialwrt[u]{t} + \bracket{\frac{1}{2} u^2} &= g a \cos \beta - \frac{\Pi}{\rho} + f(t) \tag{5} \\ \end{align*}$$ At the same time at $B$, we have $s=\frac{1}{2} \pi a, y= a, p= \Pi$. Hence Eq(2) gives $$ \frac{1}{2}\pi a\partialwrt[u]{t} + \bracket{\frac{1}{2} u^2}= ga - \frac{\Pi}{\rho} + f(t) \tag{6}$$ Eq(5)-Eq(6) gives $$\begin{align*} - a \beta \partialwrt[u]{t} &= ga \cos \beta - ga \\ \beta \partialwrt[u]{t} &= -g \cos \beta + g = g(1-\cos \beta ) \\ \partialwrt[u]{t} &= \frac{g}{\beta} (1-\cos \beta ) \\ \mbox{Also } u &= \partialwrt{t} \bracket{\frac{\pi}{2} -\beta}a = -a \frac{d\beta}{dt} \\ \partialwrt[u]{t} &= -a \frac{d^2 \beta}{dt^2} \\ \therefore \frac{g}{\beta} (1-\cos \beta ) &= -a \frac{d^2 \beta}{dt^2} \\ \frac{d^2 \beta}{dt^2} &= -\frac{g}{a\beta} (1-\cos \beta ) \\ \frac{d^2 \beta}{dt^2} &= -\frac{2g}{a\beta} \sin^2 \bracket{\frac{\beta}{2}} \end{align*}$$
