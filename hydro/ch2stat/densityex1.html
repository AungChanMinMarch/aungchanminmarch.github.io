When equal volumes of two substances are mixed the specific gravity of the mixture is 6, when equal weights of the same substances are mized, the specific gravity of the mixture is 4. Find the specific gravities of the substances.

Let the specific gravities of the substances be $s_1, s_2$.

When equal volumes, $V$ are mixed

$$\begin{align*} \frac{Vs_1 + Vs_2}{V+V} &= 6 \\ \frac{s_1 + s_2} {2} &= 6 \\ s_1 + s_2 &= 12 \\ s_2 &= 12 - s_1 \end{align*}$$

When eqaul weights $W$ are mixed,

$$\begin{align*} \frac{W+W}{\frac{W}{s_1} + \frac{W}{s_2}} &= 4 \\ \frac{2} {\frac{1}{s_1} + \frac{1}{s_2}} &= 4 \\ \frac{1}{s_1} + \frac{1}{s_2} &= \frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{1}{s_1} + \frac{1}{12-s_1} &= \frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{12-s_1 + s_1}{s_1 (12-s_1)} &= \frac{1}{2} \\ 24 &= s_1 (12 - s_1) = 12 s_1 - s_1^2 \\ s_1^2 - 12 s_1 + 24 &= 0 \\ s_1 &= \frac{-(-12) \pm \sqrt{(-12)^2 - 4(1)(24)}}{2(1)} \\ &= \frac{12 \pm \sqrt{48}}{2}\\ &= \frac{12 \pm 4\sqrt{3}}{2} \\ &= 6 \pm 2 \sqrt{3}\\ s_2 &= 12 -s_1 \\ &= 12 - (6 \pm 2 \sqrt{3})\\ & = 12 - 6 \mp 2\sqrt{3}\\ &= 6 \mp 2 \sqrt{3} \end{align*}$$ Hence the specific gravities of the substances are $6 + 2\sqrt{3}$ and $6 - 2 \sqrt{3}$.
