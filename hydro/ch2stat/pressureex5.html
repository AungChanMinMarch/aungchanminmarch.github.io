A tube in the form of a parabola held with its vertex downwards and axis vertical, is filled with different liquids of densities $\delta$ and $\delta'$. If the distances of the free surface of the liquids from the focus be $r$ and $r'$ respectively, show that the distance of their common surface from the focus is

$$\frac{r \delta -r' \delta'}{\delta - \delta'}$$

Let $S$ be the focus and $ZZ'$ be the directrix of the parabola. Let $2x$ be the distance of directrix from the focus. Let $M$ be the lowest point of the parabola. Then $MS=x$

Let $C$ be the common surface. Let $A$ and $B$ be the free surfaces. $SA=r$ and $SB=r'$

Let $\angle ASM = \alpha$, $\angle BSM = \beta$ and $\angle CSM = \theta$.

$$\begin{align*} 2x + r\cos(180 - \alpha) &= r \\ 2x - r\cos \alpha &= r \\ 2x - r &= r\cos \alpha \\ 2x - SC \cos \theta &= SC \\ 2x - SC &= SC \cos \theta \\ 2x - r' \cos \beta &= r' \\ 2x - r' &= r'\cos \beta \\ \end{align*}$$

The pressures must be the same on the two sides of $M$.

$$\begin{align*} \delta'g MB' &= \delta' g MC' + \delta g C'A' \\ \delta' MB' &= \delta' MC' + \delta MA' \\ \delta' (x-r' \cos \beta) &= \delta' (x-SC \cos \theta) + \delta (SC \cos \theta + r\cos (180 - \alpha)) \\ \delta' (x-r' \cos \beta) &= \delta' (x-SC \cos \theta) + \delta (2x -SC - r\cos (\alpha)) \\ \delta' (x-(2x-r')) &= \delta' (x-(2x-SC)) + \delta (2x -SC - (2x-r)) \\ \delta' (-x+r') &= \delta' (-x + SC) + \delta ( -SC +r) \\ -\delta'x+ \delta' r' &= -\delta' x + \delta SC - \delta SC + \delta r \\ \delta' r' &= \delta' SC - \delta SC + \delta r \\ \delta ' r' - \delta r &= (\delta' - \delta )SC\\ SC &= \frac{\delta ' r' - \delta r }{\delta' - \delta} \\ SC &= \frac{r\delta - r\delta'}{\delta - \delta'} \\ \end{align*}$$
