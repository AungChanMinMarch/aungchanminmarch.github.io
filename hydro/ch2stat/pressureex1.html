A small uniform tube is bent into the form of a circle whose plane is vertical; equal volumes of two fluids whose densities are $\rho, \sigma$ fill half the tube. Shew that the radius passing through the common surface makes with the vertical an angle $\theta$ given by $\tan \theta=\dfrac{\rho - \sigma}{\rho + \sigma}$.

It is given that the volume of the liquta in AB-vol. in BO. the circumference

Let $B$ be common surface

Let $AB$ and $BMC$ contain the liquid of density $\sigma$ and $\rho$ respectively.

Let $M$ be the lowest point of the tube.

Let $\angle BOM =\theta$.

\mbox{Since the volumes are equal, Volume in } AB &= \mbox{Volume in } BC\\ \mbox{arc } AB &= \mbox{arc } BC

Since two fluids fill half the tube, $\angle AOB = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$ and $\angle BOC = \dfrac{\pi}{2}$.
Hence $\angle MOC = \dfrac{\pi}{2} - \theta$

Let $a$ be the radius of the circular tube.

Since the pressure must be the same on both sides of $M$,
