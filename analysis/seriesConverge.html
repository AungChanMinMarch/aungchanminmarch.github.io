Convergent Series

A convergent series is a series whose partial sums tend to a specific number, also called a limit

Divergent Series

A divergent series is a series whose partial sums don't approach a limit.

Divergent Series typically go to $\infty$ or $-\infty$, don't approach one specific number.

\frac{1}{2^n}

The partial sums are $s_N = \sum\limits_{n=1}^{N} \frac{1}{2^n} = \frac{\frac{1}{2}(1-(\frac{1}{2})^N)}{1-\frac{1}{2}} = 1 - (\frac{1}{2})^N$

$$\Lim_{N \to \infty} s_N = \Lim_{N \to \infty} \bracket{1-(\frac{1}{2})^N} =1$$

The series converges and its sum is $1$.

2^n

The partial sums are $s_N = \sum\limits_{n=1}^{N} 2^N = \frac{2(2^N-1)}{2-1} = 2(2^N-1)$

$$\Lim_{N \to \infty} s_N = \Lim_{N \to \infty} (2(2^N-1)) = 2((\Lim_{N \to \infty}2^N)-1) \to \infty$$

The series does not approach specific number. Thus the series diverges
