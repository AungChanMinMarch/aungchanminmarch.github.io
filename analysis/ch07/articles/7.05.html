Give an example that $\{f_n'\}$ does not converge to $f'$.

Let $f(x)=\frac{\sin{nx}}{\sqrt{n}}$

Then $f'(x)=\frac{n\cos{nx}}{\sqrt{n}} = \sqrt{n}\cos{nx}$

\text{Since } \ -1 & \leq & \sin{nx} & \leq & 1 \\ \frac{-1}{\sqrt{n}} & \leq & \frac{\sin{nx}}{\sqrt{n}} & \leq & \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \\ \limit\frac{-1}{\sqrt{n}} & \leq & \limit\frac{\sin{nx}}{\sqrt{n}} & \leq & \limit\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \\ 0 & \leq & \limit f_x(x) & \leq & 0

Hence $\limit f_n(x) = 0$.

Let $f(x)=\limit f_n(x) = 0$

Then $f'(x)=0$

But $\limit f_n'(x) = \limit \sqrt{n}\cos{nx}$ does not exist.

Thus $\{f_n'\}$ does not converge to $f'$
