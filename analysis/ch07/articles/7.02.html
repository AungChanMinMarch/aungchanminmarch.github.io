For $m=1,2,3, \dots$ and $n=1,2,3,...$, let $s_{m,n} = \frac{m}{m+n}$.Are $\limit \Lim_{m \to \infty} s_{m,n}$ and $\Lim_{m \to \infty} \limit s_{m,n}$ equal?
$$\begin{align*} \Lim_{m \to \infty} s_{m,n} &= \Lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{m}{m+n} \\ &= \Lim_{m \to \infty} \frac{1}{1+\frac{n}{m}} \\ &= 1\\ \limit \Lim_{m \to \infty} s_{m,n} &= \limit 1 = 1\\ \limit s_{m,n} &= \limit \frac{m}{m+n} = 0\\ \Lim_{m \to \infty} \limit s_{m,n} &= \Lim_{m \to \infty} 0 = 0 \end{align*}$$ Hence they are not equal.
