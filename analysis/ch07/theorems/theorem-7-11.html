Suppose $f_n \to f$ uniformly on a set $E$ in a metric space. Let $x$ be a limit point of $E$, and suppose that $\lim\limits_{t\to x} f_n(t) = A_n$.

Then $\{A_n \}$ converges and $\lim\limits_{t \to x} f(t) = \limit A_n$. In other wrods, $\lim\limits_{t \to x} \limit f_n(t) = \limit \lim\limits_{t\to x} f_n(t) $

Let $\ep >0$.

Since $f_n \to f$ unifromly, by 7.8, there is an integer N such that $$n\geq N, m \geq N, t \in E \mbox{ implies } \abs{f_n(t) - f_m(t)} \leq \ep$$

Letting $t \to x$ in above inequility,

$$\begin{align*} \abs{\Lim_{t \to x} f_n(t) - \Lim_{t \to x} f_m(t)} &\leq \ep\\ \abs{A_n - A_m} &\leq \ep\\ \end{align*}$$

Hence $\{ A_n \}$ is a Cauchy sequence. By 3.11, $\{A_n \}$ converges.

Let $A_n \to A$.

$$\begin{align*} \abs{f(t) -A} &= \abs{f(t) - f_n(t) + f_n(t) - A_n + A_n -A}\\ \abs{f(t) -A} &\leq \abs{f_n(t) - f(t)} + \abs{f_n(t) - A_n} + \abs{A_n -A} \tag{1} \end{align*}$$

Since $f_n \to f$ uniformly and $A_n \to A$, there is an integer $N$ such that $n\geq N, t \in E$ implies $\abs{f_n(t) -f(t)} \leq \frac{\ep}{3}$ and $\abs{A_n -A} \leq \frac{\ep}{3}$

For this $n$, choose a neighborhood $V$ of $x$ such that

$$ t \in V \cap E, t \neq x \mbox{ implies } \abs{f_n(t) -A_n} \leq \frac{\ep}{3} $$ $$\begin{align*} \mbox{Now (1) gives} \abs{f(t) - A} &\leq \ep \\ \Lim_{t \to x} f(t) &= A\\ \Lim_{t \to x} f(t) &= \limit A_n\\ \Lim_{t \to x} \limit f_n(t) &= \limit \lim\limits_{t\to x} f_n(t)\\ \end{align*}$$
