Suppose $K$ is compact, and

  1. $\{f_n\}$ is a sequence of continuous functions on $K$,
  2. $\{f_n\}$ converges pointwise to a continuous function $f$ on $K$.
  3. $f_n(x) \geq f_{n+1}(x)$ for all $x \in K, n=1,2,3, \dots$
Then $f_n \to f$ uniformly on $K$.

Put $g_n = f_n -f$ and $\ep >0$.

Let $K_n = \{x \in K \mid g_n(x) \geq \ep \}$

Since $f_n$ and $f$ are continuous on $K$, $g_n$ is continuous on $K$.

Hence by theorem 4.8, $K_n$ is closed. Hence by theorem 2.35, $K_n$ is compact.

$$\begin{align*} \mbox{Since } f_n(x) &\geq f_{n+1}(x) \mbox{for all } x \in K, n=1,2,3, \dots \\ f_n(x) -f(x) &\geq f_{n+1}(x) -f(x) \mbox{ for all } x \in K, n=1,2,3, \dots,\\ g_n(x) &\geq g_{n+1}(x) \mbox{for all }x \in K, n=1,2,3, \dots,\\ \mbox{If } x &\in K_{n+1},\\ g_{n+1}(x) &\geq \ep \\ g_n(x) \geq g_{n+1}(x) &\geq \ep \\ g_n(x) &\geq \ep\\ x &\in K_n \\ K_{n+1} &\subset K_n \end{align*}$$ $$\begin{align*} \limit g_n(x) &= \limit (f_n-f)(x) \\ &= \limit (f_n(x) - f(x))\\ &= \limit f_n(x)- \limit f(x) \\ &= f(x) - f(x) \\ &= 0 \\ \therefore g_n &\to 0 \mbox{ pointwise. } \end{align*}$$

Fix $x \in K$. Since $g_n(x) \to 0$, $x \notin K_n$ if $n \to \infty$. Thus $x \notin \cap K_n$ and $\cap K_n$ is empty.
