Suppose $\{f_n\}$ is a sequence of functions defined on $E$, and suppose $\abs{f_n(x)} \leq M_n \ (x\in E, n=1,2,3, \dots)$

Then $\sum f_n$ converges uniformly on $E$ if $\sum M_n$ converges.

Let $\{s_n\}$ be the sequence of the partial sums defined by $\Sum_{i=1}^{n} f_i(x) = s_n(x)$.

Since $\sum M_n$ converges, by 3.22, for any $\ep > 0 $, there exists integer $N$ such that

$$m \geq n \geq N \mbox{ implies that } \abs{\sum_{i=n}^m M_i} \leq \ep$$ $$ \abs{\sum_{i=n+1}^{m} M_i} \leq \abs{\sum_{i=n}^m M_i} \leq \ep$$

Since $\abs{s_m(x) - s_n(x)} = \abs{\Sum_{i=1}^{m} f_i(x) - \Sum_{i=1}^{n} f_i(x)} = \abs{\Sum_{i=n+1}^{m} f_i(x)} \leq \Sum_{i=n+1}^m \abs{f_i(x)}$,

$$\begin{align*} x \in E &\mbox{ implies that }& \abs{f_n(x)} &\leq M_n \\ x \in E \mbox{ implies that }& \sum_{i=n+1}^m \abs{f_i(x)} &\leq \sum_{i=n+1}^m M_i \leq \sum_{i=n}^m M_i \\ x \in E, m \geq n \geq N &\mbox{ implies that }& \sum_{i=n+1}^m \abs{f_i(x)} &\leq \ep \\ \therefore x \in E, m, n \geq N &\mbox{ implies that }& \abs{s_m(x) - s_n(x)} &\leq \ep \\ \end{align*}$$

Hence the sequence $\{s_n\}$ converges uniformly. Since sequence of partial sums converge uniformly, $\sum f_n$ converges uniformly.
