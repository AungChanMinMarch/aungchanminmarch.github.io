Uniform Convergence

A sequence of functions $\{f_n\}, n=1,2,2,\dots$, converges uniformly on $E$ to a function $f$ if for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there is an integer $N$ such that $n>N$ implies $\mod{f_n(x)-f(x)} \leq \varepsilon$ for all $x \in E$

A series $\sum f_n(x)$ converges uniformly on $E$ if the sequence $\{s_n\}$ of partial sums defined by $s_n(x)=\Sum_{i=0}^{n}f_i(x)$ converges uniformly on $E$
